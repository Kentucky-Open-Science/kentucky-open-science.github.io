Welcome to the Archive

A curated registry of the consumer artifacts & online subcultures that defined the dot-com era. For research, education, and severe nostalgia.

The Web Archive Project is a non-profit corporate initiative dedicated to preserving the look, feel, and beige plastic of the late 1990s and early 2000s. We catalog the gadgets you owned, the websites you visited after dinner, and the screen names you regret. Our holdings are organized into two principal collections:

  • Tech & Gadgets — portable music, handheld games, dial-up hardware, and the original mobile phone.
  • Internet & Pop Culture — instant messaging, file sharing, classroom accessories, and questionable fashion.

UNDER CONSTRUCTION   New holdings are added on the second Tuesday of every month, modem permitting.

About This Site

WebArchive.com is operated by a single curator out of pure sentimentality. There is no shop. There is no checkout. The "More Info" buttons go to additional pages. The dot-com bubble burst, but we kept the website online out of spite.

Please sign our guestbook on your way out. Tell us your first AOL screen name. We promise not to laugh.
